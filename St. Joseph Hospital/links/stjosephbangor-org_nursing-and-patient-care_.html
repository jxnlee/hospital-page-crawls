Nursing and Patient Care

Our Nurses Make a Difference

At St. Joseph Healthcare, we equip and empower our nurses to pursue their calling and improve patient care by advancing our Nursing Professional Practice model through Shared Governance. As one of the largest segments of our workforce, nurses are a critical part of our team and our efforts to transform care for our community.

A Legacy of Leadership, Care and Compassion

When you join the St. Joseph Healthcare nursing team, you become part of a proud family of nurses who’ve been making an impact in Bangor and beyond for three quarters of a century. As an organization, we believe nurses are critical to our Mission and future. As health care delivery changes and evolves, our nurses continue to fuel quality, innovation and an unwavering commitment to compassion and servant leadership.

Ways We Support Our Nurses and Advance Professional Practice

We invite you to click one of the icons below to learn more about the ways St. Joseph Healthcare supports, equips and empowers nurses to care for patients, advance nursing professional practice, and grow professionally as leaders in the community and in their field.

Nurse Recognition

The Daisy Award recognizes the compassionate care and clinical excellence our nurses bring to patients every day. Learn more about how you can share your story and say thank you to a nurse by nominating them for the award.

CNA Recognition

This award recognizes Certified Nursing Assistants (CNAs) who brighten the days of their patients, families, and co-workers at St. Joseph Hospital. Honorees are selected based on demonstration of the St. Joseph Hospital Values of Compassion, Integrity, Collaboration, and Excellence.

Join Our Nursing Family

If you want to join a Mission-driven organization and dedicated nursing team that is evolving and leading the way to an innovative future, look no further than a career in nursing at St. Joseph Healthcare.

As an organization, we are committed to providing our nurses with meaningful opportunities to make a difference in the lives of patients and in the community; play an active role in shared governance; advance Nursing Professional Practice; and grow knowledge, skills and leadership abilities through personal and professional development along their career path.
